Our Background

Tropical Initiative for Communities and Women Empowerment (TICOWE) is a Community Based Organization, Non-Profit and Non-Governmental initiated in October 2019 to help spearhead community driven development initiatives and enable needy community groups participate in integrated activities to achieve quality livelihood and be self-sustainable.

TICOWE delivers its activities through the power and spirit of volunteerism approach to build sustainable communities/intercultural exchange and thriving villages in our country.

Our main purpose is promote youth employment and information exchange among Kenyans and international communities (voluntary work-camps, short and long term volunteering) youth mobility, social inclusion, sports and physical activities, community development, promotion of peace among our diverse communities, understanding and unity of purpose.


TICOWE harnesses and develop the power of young people, by effectively involving them in direct implementation of the core activities that develops their skills, enabling them become self-reliance and sustainable people in community service and development.

The Tropical Initiative for Communities and Women Empowerment (TICOWE) is based in Ndhiwa Sub County of Homa Bay County, Nyanza Region of Kenya.

A better future and right livelihood for families and individuals in our villages and self-sustainable communities.

Our mission is to improve the quality of life of the local communities through sustainable local development initiatives in Kenya.

1. People are at the very center and heart of development; they are the forces behind all we do and every endeavor we undertake.

2. Transparency and accountability drive our actions.

3. Sustainability is built into every intervention we pursue.

4. A holistic approach is essential to our treatment of ourselves and one another, with the aim of developing the whole person: health body, healthy mind, and healthy spirit

Our goal is to support the local children, youths and women through capacity building activities and empowerment to achieve knowledge, skills, networks and motivation that will make them aim to have an improved acceptable living standard and gain human dignity and self-esteem.

Our Collaborations

We work with various people and still open to more collaborations.
Some of our collaborations are as follows:

1. The organization partners with the Constituency Aids Control Council to facilitate the sharing of HIV and AIDS information to the community.


2. The organization also partners with the County Youth Affairs and Sports Office- enhancement of youth sports centre at Kakako.


3. District Children Department on guidance and development of safe child environment.


4. NOVOK-National Organization of Volunteers and Charity Workers in Kenya- Through human right education and monitoring of human right abuses in the community.


5. Ministry of Social Services- Through Social Development approaches in Homa Bay Sub-county.
